# Sourdough Parmesan Bread with Rosemary

> A fragrant, flavorful sourdough loaf studded with parmesan and fresh rosemary. Can be shaped as a boule, batard, or wreath (couronne). Perfect alongside soup, pasta, or a charcuterie board.
>
> *Recipe by Kate Freebairn — [The Pantry Mama](https://pantrymama.com/sourdough-parmesan-bread-with-rosemary/)*

## Equipment

- Mixing bowl (glass recommended)
- Digital scales
- Banneton
- Dutch oven
- Dough scraper (optional but helpful)

---

## Ingredients

| Amount | Ingredient |
|---|---|
| 500 g | Bread flour |
| 350 g | Water |
| 50 g | Sourdough starter, fed and bubbly |
| 150 g | Parmesan cheese, shaved or shredded |
| 10 g | Salt (adjust to taste) |
| 1 sprig | Fresh rosemary, leaves stripped from stalk |

---

## Instructions

### 1. Autolyse

1. Weigh out your sourdough starter and water into a large bowl and mix briefly.
2. Add the flour and salt and mix together until a shaggy dough forms. A dough scraper works well here.
3. Cover with cling film or a damp tea towel and let rest for **1 hour**.

### 2. Form the Dough

1. After autolyse, work around the bowl grabbing dough from the outside, stretching it up and over itself into the centre until a smooth ball forms (about 20–25 stretches).
2. Cover and rest for **30 minutes**.

### 3. Stretch & Fold + Add Parmesan & Rosemary

1. Perform **4–6 sets** of stretch and folds over the next 2 hours, with roughly 15 minutes between each set.
2. During the **second set**, incorporate the shaved parmesan and fresh rosemary leaves into the dough.

> **Tip:** Spray your surface with water mist rather than flour if doing stretch and folds on the bench. Wet hands prevent sticking.

### 4. Bulk Ferment

Cover the dough and allow it to ferment at room temperature until it has **just doubled in size**. It should be wobbly, bubbly, and show large air bubbles beneath the surface.

- Warm kitchen: a few hours
- Cool kitchen: may take overnight

> **Do not allow the dough to go beyond doubled** — this will result in over-fermentation.

### 5. Shape the Dough

Shape the dough into a **boule**, **batard**, or **couronne** (wreath):

- **Wreath variation:** Divide dough into 6 equal pieces (~150 g each). Shape each into a small boule and place them touching each other in a couronne banneton.

### 6. Place in Banneton

Place shaped dough into your banneton **smooth side down** (seam on top) so the bottom develops the lined pattern. Dust with rice flour as needed, handling gently to preserve the air bubbles.

### 7. Cold Ferment

Cover loosely with a plastic bag or damp tea towel and refrigerate for a **minimum of 5 hours, up to 36 hours**. Longer cold fermentation produces better blisters, crust, and flavour depth.

### 8. Preheat Oven

Preheat oven to **230°C / 450°F** with the Dutch oven inside. Preheat for at least **1 hour** for best results. Leave dough in the fridge until the very last moment.

### 9. Bake

1. Remove dough from fridge and gently turn onto a piece of baking paper.
2. Score the dough with a lame, razor blade, or knife — at minimum a deep cross. Score generously so the dough opens well.
3. Use the baking paper as a sling to lower the dough into the hot Dutch oven.
4. Optional: spritz with water before putting the lid on.

| Phase | Temp | Time |
|---|---|---|
| Lid **on** | 230°C / 450°F | 30 minutes |
| Lid **off** | 210°C / 410°F | 10–15 minutes |

### 10. Cool

Remove the loaf from the Dutch oven immediately and place on a wire rack. Allow to cool for **at least 4–6 hours** before slicing.

## Notes

- **Flour:** Written for strong bread flour. If using all-purpose flour, reduce water to account for lower protein content.
- **Starter:** Should be active and at peak — fed a few hours before baking.
- **Dried herbs:** Can substitute dried rosemary, but use no more than 2 tablespoons or the flavour will be overpowering.
- **Burnt base:** Place a baking sheet on the shelf below the Dutch oven to prevent the bottom from overbrowning.
- **Underbaked concern:** Turn oven off, place loaf directly on oven rack, leave door slightly ajar for a few hours. Do not close the door or the crust will go soggy.

---

## Flavor Variations

- Swap rosemary for **fresh thyme or parsley**
- Brush with **garlic butter** straight out of the oven
- Fill the centre of a wreath loaf with **cheese, olives, or your favourite dip**
- Substitute with **shredded Italian cheese blend** in place of parmesan

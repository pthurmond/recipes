# 🥘 Creamy Tuscan White Bean Skillet (Vegan)

> A plant-based spin on the original, swapping heavy cream for full-fat coconut milk and skipping the Parmesan entirely. The coconut milk adds richness without dairy, and nutritional yeast picks up some of that savory depth the cheese would have provided.
>
> *Adapted from [pthurmond/recipes](https://github.com/pthurmond/recipes/blob/master/Creamy-Tuscan-White-Bean-Skillet.md)*

## Ingredients

| Amount | Ingredient |
|---|---|
| 6 tbsp | Extra-virgin olive oil, divided |
| 4 cloves | Garlic, finely chopped |
| 2 cups | Cherry tomatoes, halved |
| 74 g | Red cabbage, roughly chopped |
| 4 cups (~4.25 oz) | Fresh spinach |
| 2 cans (14.5 oz each) | Cannellini beans, drained and rinsed *(see bean options below)* |
| 1 can (13.5 oz) | Full-fat coconut milk |
| 2 tbsp | Nutritional yeast |
| 1½ tsp | Kosher salt, divided |
| 1 tbsp | Aleppo pepper |
| ¼ cup | Water |
| ½ cup | Fresh basil leaves, thinly sliced, plus more torn for serving |
| 4 slices | Thick-cut crusty bread (ensure vegan — no egg or dairy) |

> **Bean options:** This recipe works with any of the following — same quantity (2 cans), same prep (drained and rinsed):
> - **Cannellini beans** *(default)* — mild, creamy, and buttery; melt into the sauce beautifully
> - **Garbanzo beans (chickpeas)** — firmer texture with a nuttier flavor; hold their shape better through simmering
> - **Black beans** — earthier and slightly denser; add a bolder flavor and a darker color to the sauce
>
> **Coconut milk tip:** Use full-fat, not lite — it's what gives the sauce its body and creaminess. Give the can a good shake or stir before using, as it separates when stored.
>
> **Nutritional yeast:** Adds a savory, slightly cheesy flavor without any dairy. Find it in the bulk or health food section of most grocery stores.

---

## Directions

1. **Heat the base:** In a large skillet over medium heat, warm **2 tbsp oil**. Add garlic and cook, stirring, until fragrant — about **1 minute**.

2. **Cook the veggies:** Add cherry tomatoes and red cabbage, season with **½ tsp salt**, and cook, tossing occasionally, until the tomatoes soften and release their juices — about **3–4 minutes**. Add spinach and cook until wilted — about **1–2 minutes**.

3. **Build the sauce:** Stir in cannellini beans, coconut milk, nutritional yeast, Aleppo pepper, remaining **1 tsp salt**, and **¼ cup water**. Bring to a boil over high heat. Reduce heat to medium-low and simmer, stirring occasionally, until thick and creamy — about **8–12 minutes**. The sauce will continue to thicken as it cools.

4. **Toast the bread:** Meanwhile, in a separate skillet over medium-high heat, warm **2 tbsp oil**. Add 2 slices of bread and cook until golden brown on both sides — about **3–4 minutes total**. Transfer to a plate. Repeat with remaining bread and **2 tbsp oil**.

5. **Finish & serve:** Fold the basil into the bean mixture. Taste and adjust salt if needed. Top with torn basil leaves and serve with toasted bread on the side.

## Vegan Substitutions Made

| Original | Vegan Swap | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| 1 cup heavy cream | 1 can (13.5 oz) full-fat coconut milk | Provides similar richness; slight coconut flavor works well with Tuscan spices |
| 1 oz Parmesan | 2 tbsp nutritional yeast | Adds savory/umami depth; increase to taste |

## Notes & Variations

- **Coconut flavor too prominent?** A squeeze of lemon juice at the end brightens the dish and helps balance it.
- **More protein:** Stir in a second can of beans or add a handful of cooked lentils.
- **Greens swap:** Kale or chard work well in place of spinach — just add them a minute or two earlier since they're sturdier.
- **Spice level:** Aleppo pepper is mild and fruity. Sub smoked paprika for a smokier profile, or crushed red pepper if you want more heat.
- **Serving without bread:** Spoon over cooked farro, polenta, or pasta for a heartier meal.
- **Storage:** Keeps well in the fridge for up to 3 days. The sauce thickens considerably when cold — add a splash of water when reheating.

# Sourdough Cinnamon Raisin Bread with Vanilla-Soaked Raisins

> A soft, enriched sourdough sandwich loaf with a cinnamon-sugar swirl and vanilla-soaked raisins. Great toasted with cream cheese or butter, and makes excellent French toast the next day.
>
> *Recipe by Emilie Raffa — [The Clever Carrot](https://www.theclevercarrot.com/2020/08/sourdough-cinnamon-raisin-bread/)*

---

## Details

| | |
|---|---|
| **Method** | Stand mixer (or by hand) |
| **Bulk Rise** | 8–12 hours @ 68°F (overnight) |
| **Second Rise** | 1.5–2 hours |
| **Bake Time** | 45–50 minutes @ 375°F |
| **Yield** | 1 loaf |

### Baking Schedule

This is an overnight dough. Start the evening before and plan to bake the following day.

- **Cool kitchen (~68°F):** Overnight bulk rise, 8–12 hours
- **Warm kitchen (80°F+):** Bulk rise during the day, 4–6 hours — bake in the afternoon or evening

---

## Equipment

- Stand mixer with paddle and dough hook attachments (or mix by hand)
- 8.5 x 4-inch loaf pan
- Rolling pin
- Measuring tape (helpful for shaping)

---

## Ingredients

### Dough

| Amount | Ingredient |
|---|---|
| 500 g (4 cups) | Bread flour |
| 85 g (6 tbsp) | Unsalted butter, softened and cut into cubes |
| 24 g (2 tbsp) | Sugar |
| 9 g | Fine sea salt |
| 100 g (½ cup) | Bubbly, active sourdough starter (100% hydration) |
| 240 g (1 cup) | Warm water |
| 85 g (½ cup) | Raisins |
| 1 tbsp | Pure vanilla extract (for soaking the raisins) |
| 1 | Egg + splash of water, beaten (egg wash) |

> **Faster rise option:** Use 150 g sourdough starter + 230 g warm water, keeping all other quantities the same.
>
> **Egg wash substitute:** Plain water works fine in place of egg wash.

### Cinnamon-Sugar Swirl

| Amount | Ingredient |
|---|---|
| ⅓ cup | Sugar |
| 1 tbsp | Ground cinnamon |
| 1 tbsp | All-purpose flour |

> **Note:** The flour in the swirl helps bind the filling and reduce gaps between the swirl and the bread.

---

## Instructions

### 1. Mix the Dough

1. Add flour, butter, sugar, and salt to the bowl of a stand mixer fitted with the paddle attachment. Mix on low speed until the butter resembles coarse crumbs.
2. Add the starter and warm water. Mix until the flour is fully absorbed.
3. Cover with plastic wrap or a damp towel and rest for **30 minutes**.
4. While the dough rests, soak the raisins in the vanilla extract. Drain and squeeze dry in a paper towel before using.
5. After the rest, add the raisins to the bowl. Switch to the dough hook and knead on medium-low speed for **6–8 minutes** until soft and supple. If sticky, add a small amount of flour.

**By hand:** Knead the dough *without the raisins* for 8–10 minutes until smooth and elastic. Cover and rest for 1 hour. Add the raisins during your first stretch and fold — they incorporate much more easily at this stage.

---

### 2. Bulk Rise with Stretch & Folds

1. Cover the bowl and let the dough rise overnight at room temperature until doubled in size (~8–12 hours at 68°F).
2. Begin stretch and folds **30–60 minutes into the bulk rise**:
   - Grab a portion of the dough and stretch it upward, then fold toward the center.
   - Rotate the bowl a quarter turn and repeat until you've gone full circle (4 folds = 1 set).
   - Rest 30 minutes, then do a **second set**.

---

### 3. Shape the Dough

1. Butter the loaf pan. Combine the sugar, cinnamon, and flour in a small bowl; set aside.
2. Turn the dough onto a lightly floured surface. Gently pat and stretch to release large air bubbles.
3. Roll the dough into a **6 x 20-inch rectangle** with a rolling pin.
   - The 6-inch width fits snugly in the pan.
   - The longer the roll, the more swirls you'll get.
4. Lightly score a 1-inch border around the sides and a 2-inch border at the very top (do not cut through — just mark it).
5. Brush the entire surface with egg wash or water.
6. Sprinkle the cinnamon-sugar mixture inside the scored border.
7. Slowly roll the dough into a log, pinching the ends to seal.
8. Place into the loaf pan **seam side down**.

> **Tip on gaps:** Gaps between the swirl and bread can form from steam during baking. They don't affect flavor. To minimize them, shape carefully and avoid over-proofing in the second rise.

---

### 4. Second Rise

1. Cover the pan and let rest at room temperature until the dough rises to **about 1 inch above the rim** of the pan — approximately 1.5–2 hours.
2. The dough should look soft and slightly puffy, not dense.
3. Preheat oven to **375°F (190°C)** toward the end of the second rise.

---

### 5. Bake

1. Place the loaf pan on the **center rack** and bake for **45–50 minutes**, or until golden brown.
2. If the top browns too quickly, tent loosely with aluminum foil around the 20-minute mark.
3. Cool in the pan for at least **10 minutes**, then transfer to a wire rack to cool completely.

---

## Storage

Stays fresh for up to **3 days** in a plastic bag at room temperature. Freezes well — slice before freezing for easy single-serving toasting.

---

## Serving Suggestions

- Toasted with melted butter or cream cheese
- French toast the next day
- Sliced and frozen for weekday breakfasts

---

## Variations & Tips

- **Coconut sugar** can be substituted for regular sugar in the dough.
- **Dried cranberries, tart cherries, or golden raisins** work well in place of regular raisins.
- For a **richer swirl**, use brown sugar and butter in the filling instead of the standard cinnamon-sugar.
- Add **chopped pecans or walnuts** to the filling for crunch.
- For **dairy-free**, substitute plant-based butter (avocado oil-based sticks work well).
- If using a **glass loaf pan**, reduce oven temperature by 25°F and add about 20 minutes to the bake time.
- **Cold retard option:** After shaping, cover and refrigerate overnight instead of doing the second rise at room temperature. Bake straight from the fridge the next morning.
